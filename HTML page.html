Benjamin Solomon Carson Sr.

American neurosurgeon and politician
Timeline of Dr. Ben Carson
It does not matter where we come from or what we look like. If we recognize our abilities, are willing to learn and to use what we know in helping others, we will always have a place in the world. -Ben Carson
  • In 1982,Ben Carson was appointed the chief resident in neurosurgery at Johns Hopkins
  • In 1985 Dr. Ben Carson became the Director of Pediatric Neurosurgery at Johns Hopkins at the age of 33.
  • On September 4th, 1987 Dr. Ben Carson performed surgery on the Binder twins, who were conjoined at the head.
  • Carson's best-selling autobiography, "Gifted Hands: The Ben Carson Story," was published in 1990
  • In 1994, he founded the Carson Scholars Fund with his wife, which facilitates leisure reading for children and funds college scholarships for students with strong academics and humanitarian achievement.
  • In 1997, Dr. Ben Carson was the primary neurosurgeon on the team that performs the first fully successful surgery to separate Type 2 vertical craniopagus twins (joined at the top of the head and facing opposite directions), where both twins survive and are neurologically normal.
  • He co-founded the Benevolent Endowment Network (BEN) Fund, which provides financial support for the medical expenses of pediatric neurosurgery patients in 2002.
  • He received the Presidential Medal of Freedom from President Bush on June 19, 2008
  • He retired from Johns Hopkins as director of pediatric neurosurgery, professor and co-director of the Craniofacial Center July 1, 2013.
  • Before retirement, Carson was reported to have performed over 300 surgeries a year, almost three times the average of any neurosurgeon
